Why This Matters

Homelessness and food insecurity are growing issues affecting millions of people both globally and within our own communities. In the United States alone, many families struggle to access basic necessities such as food and shelter. This project was created to raise awareness, educate others, and encourage action. By combining research, community involvement, and advocacy, this website highlights the reality of poverty while showing that even small efforts can create meaningful change.

The Reality

Hunger and homelessness are not rare problems. They affect millions of people every day and are made worse by rising living costs, conflict, and economic instability.

17 Million

U.S. households experienced food insecurity.

735 Million

People globally face chronic hunger.

650,000+

People in the U.S. experience homelessness.

1 in 4

Children worldwide live in severe child food poverty.

Chicago Impact

Food insecurity and homelessness are not only global issues. They are happening right here in Chicago. Thousands of individuals and families, including students, face housing instability and rely on shelters and food pantries for survival. Local organizations play an important role in supporting these individuals, especially during the winter months. Focusing on Chicago makes the issue more personal and shows how communities can work together to support real solutions.

Be Part of the Solution

Solving homelessness and food insecurity requires collective effort. Everyone can help in some way.

  • Volunteer at local shelters and food pantries
  • Donate food, clothing, and hygiene products
  • Raise awareness through conversations and social media
  • Support organizations working to help families in need

Reflection

This project showed that homelessness and food insecurity are complex problems that require both immediate support and long-term solutions. Researching these issues helped deepen understanding of how poverty affects individuals, families, and children on both a local and global level. It also demonstrated that awareness matters because education can inspire action, increase community involvement, and encourage sustainable support for shelters and food programs.
